My Story

“Far better it is to dare mighty things, to win glorious triumphs, even though checkered by failure, than to take rank with those poor spirits who neither enjoy much nor suffer much, because they live in the gray twilight that knows neither victory nor defeat.” ― Theodore Roosevelt

Here are a few things about me:

  • I created an eCommerce store that allowed me to pay for college and travel. I sold over 20,000 products bringing in a steady income.
  • I quit college because I could not figure out what direction I wanted to take and I was paying for out of pocket. I was having trouble figuring out what to do.
  • I spend 3 years as a promo model for trade shows, music festivals (Coachella), and conventions (Comic Con)
  • I started a business as a broker for telecom, energy, and merchant services. I learned public speaking, sales negotiation, and the importance of personal development.
  • 2017 was the year that I officially became a female in tech. I earned a scholarship to an IT boot-camp. I attained my certifications and started my career.
  • Within one year I had reached my goal to be in Information Security.

I mention all this here, just to clarify that when I talk about business and being a female in tech, I do so from actual experience.

My basic philosophy is all about developing the right mindset not just technical skills they can only get you so far.

What is this website about?

The foundation of any success is having the proper mindset, character, and work ethic. Having the techniques, tools, and skills are just not enough. You cannot start a successful career in tech without developing the proper mindset to fortify your mind. This site is to empower women in tech by sharing the marketing and business strategies needed along with developing the proper mindset to become a bulletproof female in tech.

Least Likely to Succeed: My Story

I was born in in a third world country, raised by immigrant parents, and did not know a lick of English when I arrived to the U.S. at the age of 4. Growing up I did not have the opportunities that those who are born in the country do such as getting a job, a drivers license, and a financed college education. I felt like the odds were stacked against me because I was not a U.S. citizen.

4 year old me when I first arrived to the U.S.

Life After High School

In 2010 I graduated high school. I was deeply depressed, unhappy, and hopeless. I had no future and would never amount to anything in life. While my classmates were attending college, landing great jobs, and dating I was sleeping my days away just to escape from reality. I felt like I was stuck in a mental nightmare constantly thinking: how will I make money if I can't even get a job? What will I do for the rest of my life? Will I ever have a normal life? What is the point of being in this country if I can not do anything? Why was I even born? I'd never been so lost and afraid.

A Blessing In Disguise

I purchased an Xbox gaming console at a really good price from Craigslist that came with all sorts of video games, controllers, and gear but to my surprise it was actually broken. The console had the infamous red ring of death (basically the console had overheated). I purchased a defective console. I had been scammed. I was infuriated. I had spent the little money I did have on a defective console. After several attempts to contact the seller with no response I decided to do research and see if I could fix it. With the help of my dad I was able to successfully fix the console and sell it along with all the gear for 4x the amount that I purchased it for.

Then I Thought Of Something Could Do

I could start a business. From this event a business idea had been born. I bought Xbox consoles that had the same problem, fix them, and flip them for a profit. I started an eCommerce store which meant I could pay for college and actually have a shot at life. Having a business meant I had control over my future. For once life was looking promising.

Xbox Arcade Red Ring of Death                                                      Trade show in Las Vegas, NV representing Hyundai

My First, Tiny Taste of Progress

I quickly encountered a bottleneck in fixing and reselling Xbox consoles: me! It was very time consuming process in fixing Xbox consoles. Plus profit margins had dwindled when new Xbox versions were released. I transition my eCommerce store to a drop-shipping model. By this point my store was doing very well. I used the money from my eCommerce store to fund for college with no real idea on what I wanted to study. After some time of only taking electives I stopped attending college until I knew what I wanted to study.

The Entrepreneur Bug

During this time I began promo modeling at trade shows such as SEMA, CES, ASD throughout the west coast. I would use my lunch breaks to roam the trade-show floor to gather free gadgets and meet the business owners behind them. I was amazed by all latest tech gadgets, cars, and products. I was inspired by all the different types of business ideas and opportunities. This period of time taught me how marketing works, brand development, and how to speak confidently to strangers. I spoke to small business owners and trade-show attendees. I was curious to learn the story behind these successful products, the business owners, and how the business functioned. I took a couple of them out for lunch and picked their brains. Their stories inspired me. But most importantly it gave me hope.

Opportunities

It is said that people's number one fear is public speaking. Number two is death. The fear of public speaking is very real. I partnered with a telecom company that recently had expanded to Latin America. I opened a business under them as a broker for their services. The company was set up as teams and out of my team I was the only one who spoke fluent Spanish. Therefore, I was the only one who could carry out the business presentations. I spent countless hours reading books about sales, public speaking, and marketing. I joined Toastmaster groups and even signed up for Improv. I figured that if I could embarrass myself on stage then maybe the pain of public speaking would not be so bad. I immersed myself in personal development and the success stories of others to strengthen my mindset. This venture proved to me that I was capable of more than what I ever thought I was capable. It ignited a hunger for knowledge a fire inside of me, a hunger to do and be more.

My Jump to Tech

I did not choose tech, it choose me. I found an ad on Craigslist for a scholarship opportunity to attend an IT boot-camp. I knew nothing about tech especially about Information Security. I didn't even know Information Security existed! I had a friend that was already in the tech field working in Information Security. He sparked my curiosity and interest for the field. I saw how successful he was without earning a college degree, he worked from home, and earned six figures. But what really caught my eye was that I could use my curiosity for learning in an ever changing always evolving industry filled with problems to solve. This has created such a high demand for more people to enter the field giving me the opportunity to use my work ethic to squeeze in without needing a degree. After several interviews I earned the scholarship for the IT boot-camp and I jumped in head first. I spend my days coding, taking computers apart, and studying for my certifications. Even after countless of hours, long nights, and weekends of studying I still managed to fail at my first attempt at the certificate exam. I went in for a second shot, I passed, and graduated from the IT tech boot-camp. This experience really challenged me especially because I had no prior IT knowledge what so ever.

My tech boot camp graduation

My Break In Information Security

I knew since day one of my tech journey that I wanted to be in Information Security. I spent my weekends setting up virtual machines with vulnerability scanners, setting up servers, and studying for my security certification. I did my best to not miss any Information Security networking events in my area. I was constantly networking. While working in desktop support I would use my lunch breaks to hang out with the Information Security team in the company I was working in and pick their brains. I did this with every job I had. I was constantly learning. Then finally after one year in the tech industry I landed my first job in Information Security. I could not believe it. But now what?

Where it All Comes Together

Throughout my tech journey I could not help to always notice how I was always the only female in the room. I stood out like a sore thumb in every way possible. I knew since day one that this would be the case. But I also knew that this was my slight edge. Along my journey I documented the struggles, fears, and experiences that I faced because I knew that it would be of value one day to someone especially women. My ultimate goal is to make a difference. I want to contribute something of value and leave the world better than how I found it. I don't know what form that will take yet. If this can inspire just one person then I have achieved my goal. This website is where all my experiences comes together to empower women in tech. That if I can do it then anybody can. Women in tech will be the new norm.
